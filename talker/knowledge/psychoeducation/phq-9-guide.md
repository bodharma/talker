# PHQ-9: Patient Health Questionnaire-9

## What the PHQ-9 Measures

The PHQ-9 is a nine-item screening instrument that measures the severity of depressive symptoms over the past two weeks. Each item corresponds directly to one of the nine diagnostic criteria for major depressive disorder as defined in the DSM-5. It is one of the most widely used and well-validated depression screening tools in clinical practice, used in primary care, mental health settings, and research worldwide.

The nine items assess: loss of interest or pleasure, depressed mood, sleep disturbance, fatigue or low energy, appetite changes, feelings of worthlessness or guilt, difficulty concentrating, psychomotor agitation or retardation, and thoughts of death or self-harm.

Each item is scored from 0 to 3 based on frequency: 0 (not at all), 1 (several days), 2 (more than half the days), 3 (nearly every day). The total score ranges from 0 to 27.

## Score Ranges and What They Mean

### Minimal Depression (0-4)

A score in this range suggests symptoms are absent or very mild. Most people scoring here are not experiencing clinically significant depression. Day-to-day functioning is generally unaffected. No treatment is typically indicated, though periodic rescreening can be helpful if there are known risk factors.

### Mild Depression (5-9)

Scores in this range indicate the presence of some depressive symptoms that may be noticeable but are not yet significantly impairing daily life. A person might feel more tired than usual, have occasional low mood, or notice reduced enjoyment in activities. Watchful waiting is often appropriate. Lifestyle interventions such as regular exercise, improved sleep habits, and social engagement can be beneficial. A follow-up screening in a few weeks helps track whether symptoms are stable, improving, or worsening.

### Moderate Depression (10-14)

This range represents a meaningful level of depressive symptoms that are likely affecting work performance, relationships, or daily routines. A person might find it harder to complete tasks, withdraw from social situations, or struggle with persistent low mood. Clinical evaluation is recommended at this level. Treatment options include psychotherapy, particularly cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), and in some cases medication may be considered. A treatment plan developed with a mental health professional is appropriate.

### Moderately Severe Depression (15-19)

Scores in this range indicate substantial depressive symptoms that are significantly interfering with functioning. Daily activities like getting out of bed, going to work, or maintaining relationships may feel overwhelming. Concentration difficulties and fatigue are often prominent. Active treatment is strongly recommended, typically involving psychotherapy and/or antidepressant medication. Referral to a mental health specialist is appropriate if not already involved.

### Severe Depression (20-27)

This range suggests severe depressive symptoms with major functional impairment. A person may be unable to work, care for themselves, or engage in basic daily activities. The emotional pain is intense and persistent. Immediate clinical attention is needed. Treatment usually involves a combination of psychotherapy and medication. Close monitoring is essential, and in some cases more intensive interventions such as partial hospitalization or intensive outpatient programs may be warranted.

## The Significance of Item 9

Item 9 asks about thoughts of being better off dead or of hurting oneself. This item carries special clinical importance regardless of the total score. Any positive response to item 9 (a score of 1, 2, or 3) warrants further assessment of suicidal ideation, intent, and plan.

A person who scores low overall but endorses item 9 still needs a safety evaluation. The frequency matters: someone reporting these thoughts "nearly every day" requires a more urgent response than someone reporting them "several days" in the past two weeks, but all positive responses should be explored.

When item 9 is positive, the next step is a more detailed suicide risk assessment. This includes asking about the nature of the thoughts (passive wishes to be dead versus active plans), access to means, history of suicide attempts, and protective factors. Crisis resources should be provided whenever item 9 is endorsed.

## When to Seek Help

Seeking professional evaluation is recommended when:

- The total score is 10 or above (moderate range or higher)
- Item 9 is endorsed at any level
- Symptoms have persisted for more than two weeks
- Symptoms are interfering with work, relationships, or daily functioning
- There has been a noticeable change from baseline functioning
- Someone close to you has expressed concern about your mood or behavior

A PHQ-9 score is a snapshot, not a diagnosis. It captures how things have been over the past two weeks. Scores can fluctuate, and a single elevated score does not necessarily mean major depressive disorder is present. Conversely, a low score during a "good week" does not rule out depression if the overall pattern has been concerning. The PHQ-9 is most useful as part of a broader clinical picture that includes history, context, and professional judgment.

## Tracking Over Time

One of the PHQ-9's strengths is its sensitivity to change, making it useful for monitoring treatment progress. A reduction of 5 or more points generally represents a clinically meaningful improvement. Repeated administration at regular intervals (such as every 2-4 weeks during treatment) helps clinicians and patients evaluate whether the current approach is working or needs adjustment.
