# Crisis Resources and Emergency Information

## When to Call Emergency Services (911)

Call 911 or your local emergency number immediately if someone is:

- Actively attempting to harm themselves or end their life
- In possession of a weapon or lethal means with expressed intent to use it
- Experiencing a medical emergency related to self-harm (overdose, severe injury)
- Threatening imminent violence toward others
- In a psychotic state and unable to ensure their own safety

Emergency services can dispatch mobile crisis teams in many areas, which pair mental health professionals with first responders. If available, request a crisis intervention team (CIT) trained officer.

## Crisis Hotlines and Text Lines

### United States

**988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline**
- Call or text: 988
- Available 24/7, free and confidential
- Serves anyone in suicidal crisis or emotional distress
- Specialized lines available: Veterans (press 1), Spanish (press 2), LGBTQ+ youth (press 3)
- Chat available at 988lifeline.org

**Crisis Text Line**
- Text HOME to 741741
- Available 24/7, free and confidential
- Trained crisis counselors respond via text
- Also available via WhatsApp and Facebook Messenger

**Veterans Crisis Line**
- Call 988, then press 1
- Text 838255
- Chat at veteranscrisisline.net
- Available 24/7 for veterans, service members, and their families

**SAMHSA National Helpline**
- Call 1-800-662-4357
- Available 24/7, free and confidential
- Information and referrals for substance use and mental health treatment
- Available in English and Spanish

### United Kingdom

**Samaritans**
- Call 116 123 (free, 24/7)
- Email jo@samaritans.org (response within 24 hours)
- Available to anyone in emotional distress, not limited to suicidal feelings

**SHOUT Crisis Text Line**
- Text SHOUT to 85258
- Free, confidential, 24/7

### International

**International Association for Suicide Prevention (IASP)**
- Directory of crisis centers worldwide at iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres
- Links to local crisis services in over 50 countries

**Befrienders Worldwide**
- Directory at befrienders.org
- Crisis support centers across the globe

**Crisis Lines by Country (Selected)**

## Warning Signs of Imminent Crisis

Recognizing warning signs allows for earlier intervention. The following indicators suggest someone may be in immediate danger and needs urgent support.

### Verbal Warning Signs

- Talking about wanting to die or kill themselves
- Expressing feelings of being a burden to others ("Everyone would be better off without me")
- Talking about feeling trapped or in unbearable pain
- Saying they have no reason to live or no sense of purpose
- Talking about seeking access to lethal means

### Behavioral Warning Signs

- Searching online for methods of suicide
- Giving away valued possessions
- Saying goodbye to people as if it is a final farewell
- Putting affairs in order (writing a will, settling debts) unexpectedly
- Sudden calmness after a period of severe depression (may indicate a decision has been made)
- Withdrawing from friends, family, and activities
- Increased alcohol or drug use
- Stockpiling medications
- Visiting or calling people to say goodbye

### Situational Risk Factors

- Recent major loss (death of a loved one, end of a relationship, job loss)
- Previous suicide attempt (the strongest predictor of future attempts)
- Access to lethal means, particularly firearms
- Chronic pain or terminal illness diagnosis
- Recent discharge from a psychiatric hospital (the period immediately after discharge carries elevated risk)
- Exposure to another person's suicide

## How to Help Someone in Crisis

If you believe someone is in immediate danger, do not leave them alone. Call emergency services if needed. Remove access to lethal means if it is safe to do so. Listen without judgment and let them know you are there. Ask directly: "Are you thinking about suicide?" Asking about suicide does not increase risk and can provide critical relief. Stay with the person until professional help arrives or the crisis has passed.
